Harley-Davidson

The new Harley-Davidson Forty-Eight Special sports a semi-digital console mounted on the handlebar. The bike gets an electronic speedometer with odometer, time-of-day clock, dual trip meter, low fuel warning light, low oil pressure light, engine diagnostics readout, LED indicator lights and more.

Ducati desert X

Ducati’s DesertX fills a void in Ducati’s adventure-bike lineup as a more hardcore middleweight ADV intended to tackle rougher terrain than a street-biased adventure bike. An early design goal was to ensure that the bike came equipped with 21-inch front and 18-inch rear wheel sizes, opening the door to much more aggressive knobby tire options.

Honda CB350RS

In terms of design, the new Honda CB350RS will feature contemporary styling. Thus, the front fascia gets a round, LED headlight. The cockpit includes a semi-digital instrument cluster while the seat features a tuck and roll design. Other features include Y-shaped alloy wheels, wide pattern tyres, front fork boots, full-LED lighting.

Jawa 42 Bobber

In terms of styling, the new Jawa 42 Bobber packs a minimalistic design. This includes a new round headlamp at the front, independent clock console, new handlebar, bar-end mirrors, new design for the fuel tank with tank pads, and a redesigned seat. It also packs chopped fenders, low single seat, and fat tyres.

Yamaha FZ25

The Yamaha FZ25 is a reliable and comfortable motorcycle with a smooth 249cc engine. It offers good fuel economy and handles well in city traffic and on highways. The bike's comfortable riding position and adequate braking system make it a suitable choice for both new and experienced riders.

Honda CB300F

The Honda CB300F is a well-rounded motorcycle offering a good balance of performance and comfort. Its 293cc engine delivers solid power and torque, making it suitable for both city commutes and highway cruising. The bike's comfortable ergonomics and smooth handling make it a joy to ride.

Hero Splender Plus

The Hero Splendor Plus is a reliable and fuel-efficient commuter motorcycle. Its 97.2cc engine delivers modest power and torque, making it suitable for city riding. The bike's comfortable ergonomics and light weight contribute to its easy handling. While not a performance powerhouse, the Splendor Plus excels in fuel efficiency.

Hero Glamour

The Hero Glamour is a popular commuter motorcycle known for its fuel efficiency and reliability. Its 125cc engine delivers adequate power for city riding. The bike's comfortable ergonomics and light weight make it easy to maneuver in traffic. While it may not be the most powerful in its class.

Royal Enfield Cafe racer

The Royal Enfield Continental GT 650 is a modern-day café racer that captures the spirit of the 1960s. Its 648cc parallel-twin engine delivers ample power and torque, making it a thrilling ride. The bike's lightweight chassis and well-tuned suspension provide agile handling and a dynamic riding experience.

Bajaj Pulsor

The Bajaj Pulsar series is renowned for its sporty performance and aggressive styling. Models like the Pulsar NS200 and RS200 offer powerful engines, sharp handling, and advanced features. The Pulsar 150 and 180 are popular choices for daily commuting and weekend rides, offering a good balance of performance and fuel efficiency.

Yezdi Roadster

The Yezdi Roadster is a modern classic motorcycle that blends retro aesthetics with modern performance. Its 334cc liquid-cooled engine delivers a good balance of power and torque, making it enjoyable for both city commutes and highway cruising. The bike's comfortable ergonomics and decent suspension setup.

Suzuki Hayabusa

The Suzuki Hayabusa is a legendary hyperbike known for its incredible speed and power. Its 1340cc, inline-four engine delivers a staggering 190 horsepower, propelling the bike to breathtaking speeds. The aerodynamic design and advanced electronics contribute to its exceptional performance and stability.
